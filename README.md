# My Markdown Journal
Create your own online journal, write in simple markdown, and publish for free without having to code. Works in the browser here on GitHub without needing an IDE.

## Get started writing your own blog

You don't need to be a "tech person" to use this. If you can send an email or write a text message, you can do this. Just follow these four steps:

### 1. Make this yours (The "Fork")
At the very top right of this page, look for a button that says **Fork**. 
* Click it, then click **Create Fork**. 
* This makes a personal copy of this entire project and puts it in your own GitHub account. It’s now your site!

### 2. Turn on your website
Now you need to tell GitHub to turn your code into a live website.
1. Click the **Settings** tab at the top of your screen.
2. On the left sidebar, click **Pages**.
3. Under **Branch**, click the button that says "None" and change it to **main**.
4. Click **Save**.
5. Give it about 60 seconds. At the top of that page, you’ll see a link like `https://yourname.github.io/my-journal/`. **That is your new website!**

---

### 3. Write your first post
To write a new entry, you don't need a special program. You can do it right here in your browser:
1. Go back to your main page (click the **Code** tab at the top).
2. Click on the folder named **_posts**.
3. Click the **Add file** button, then **Create new file**.
4. **Name your file:** It must follow this specific format: `YEAR-MONTH-DAY-title.md` (Example: `2026-04-09-my-first-walk.md`).
5. **Start with the "Title" tag:** At the very top of your file, copy and paste this exactly:
   ```text
   ---
   title: My Best Day Ever
   ---
   ```
6. **Write:** Type your thoughts below that. When you are done, click the green **Commit changes** button at the top right. (Think of "Commit" as "Save and Publish").

### 4. Change your Blog Name
Want to change "My Journal" to your own name?
1. Go back to your main page and click on the file named **_config.yml**.
2. Click the **Pencil icon** (edit) on the right.
3. Change the text after `title:` and `description:`.
4. Click the green **Commit changes** button.

## A Quick Guide to "Fancy Texting" (Markdown)
Markdown is just a way to add formatting using symbols you already know:

* **# Heading** (One # is a big title, ## is a smaller heading)
* **Bold text** is written like `**this**`
* *Italic text* is written like `*this*`
* **Lists** start with a dash: `- Item 1`
* **Links** look like this: `[Click here](https://google.com)`

> **Tip:** Every time you "Commit" a change, your website will automatically update. It usually takes about 30 seconds to "bake" the changes into the live site. Just refresh your browser to see the results!
